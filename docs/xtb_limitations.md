# Known xTB Limitations in the Maillard Computational Pipeline

GFN2-xTB is the chosen Tier 1 semi-empirical method for rapid energy screening. While it is incredibly efficient and accurately reproduces geometric parameters for organic molecules, there are specific limitations inherent to its theoretical formulation that impact the Maillard reaction cascade.

These must be accounted for and refined in Tier 2 (Skala/DFT).

## 1. Proton Transfer in Water
The Maillard cascade is highly dependent on proton transfer (e.g., Amadori rearrangement, enolisation).
* **Limitation:** xTB utilizes an implicit solvation model (ALPB/GBSA) and does not inherently describe the explicit participation of water molecules as proton shuttles (Grotthuss mechanism). 
* **Impact:** Uncatalyzed, implicit-solvent proton transfer barriers in xTB are often overestimated by **10–25 kcal/mol**. 
* **Mitigation:** Rate-limiting steps involving proton transfer *must* be refined in Tier 2 using explicit water molecules in the transition state. Specifically:
    - **Amadori / Heyns Rearrangement:** Requires **1 to 2** explicit H₂O molecules assisting the proton shuttle from C2 to C1.
    - **1,2- and 2,3-Enolisation:** Requires **1** explicit H₂O molecule to assist the dehydration steps.

## 2. Zwitterionic Intermediates
Amino acids exist as zwitterions (NH3+ / COO-) at typical cooking pH levels (5-8).
* **Limitation:** Semi-empirical methods often struggle to correctly balance the energy between neutral and zwitterionic tautomers compared to high-level DFT.
* **Impact:** The initial nucleophilic attack of the amine on the sugar carbonyl ring (Schiff base formation) may have slightly distorted energetics.

## 3. β-Elimination Reactions (DHA Pathway)
The decomposition of Serine and Cysteine into Dehydroalanine (DHA) is a key competitive pathway.
* **Limitation:** The barrier for breaking the C-O or C-S bond concurrently with the C-H bond in a β-elimination can be systematically under- or overestimated depending on the leaving group parametrised in GFN2-xTB.
* **Impact:** Energetics may be off by 5–15 kcal/mol, throwing off the stoichiometric competition ratio.
* **Mitigation:** DHA elimination barriers (todo: 3.2f) have been explicitly flagged for Tier 2 Skala refinement.

## 4. Spin-State Crossings (Sulfur Radicals)
Dimethyl disulfide/trisulfide formation involves radical recombination.
* **Limitation:** xTB is parametirized primarily for closed-shell ground-state organics. Open-shell radicals are supported but are generally less accurate than closed-shell species.

---
**Conclusion:** 
Tier 1 (xTB) is exceptionally fit for identifying *which* pathways are kinetically viable out of thousands generated by RMG. However, absolute barrier heights and branching ratios for decisive steps require the Tier 2 Skala module.
